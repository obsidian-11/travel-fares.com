Best Tourist Attractions in San Francisco

Golden Gate Bridge: The Golden Gate Bridge, which spans San Francisco Bay, is a California landmark. It is the town's most photogenic location, with the orange building backed by blue water or, in several situations, peaking through a low-lying cloud. The flood-lit building is equitably impressive at night. If you wish to travel over the Golden Gate Bridge, take U.S. Highway 101, also known as S.R. 1, and there are sidewalks on both sides of the bridge that are accessible to pedestrians and bicyclists. How about capturing a marvelous look of the river at the Golden Gate Bridge Vista Point which is situated on a hill overlooking San Francisco? Sounds impressive... pack your bags, now!

Alcatraz Island: One of America's most legendary jails is the ancient and violent Alcatraz prison, situated on Alcatraz Island in San Francisco Bay. It was in operation for nearly three decades, shutting in 1963 and restarting as a visitor destination in 1973. You can take a cruise ship to the island and travel the location while listening to an excellent audio tape that provides a glimpse into daily existence in the jail instead of a chronological list of facts. Former Alcatraz inmates and wardens even lend their voices to the narration. There is no fees for entry into Alcatraz but you need to purchase a ferry ticket to get to the island. Ticket price starting from $39.90 for adults.

Fisherman's Wharf: Fisherman's Wharf is a famous traveler destination in San Francisco. If you're visiting the town for the 1st time and only have a day or two to view the attractions, Fisherman's Wharf is among the top locations to go. This historic district, formerly recognized as San Francisco's Little Italy, is recognized for its stores, cafes, and picturesque setting along the riverfront. It's a pleasant spot to walk around and have a feel for the town. You can also arrange a fishing charter or take a scenic boat ride from here for stunning viewpoints of the town. The ports near Pier 39 are among the finest places in town to view sea lions.

Cable Cars: Cable cars were invented in 1873 to assist residents in navigating the town's numerous mountains. Nowadays, the few remaining cable cars provide visitors with an excellent opportunity to discover the city anciently. Since 1964, these tram-like cars have been distinguished as being the only public transportation scheme to be designated as a heritage structure. San Francisco's Cable Cars designated as a National Historic Landmark in 1964. This system is widely used by visitor to take a look around downtown San Francisco, hidden gems, and streets including the famous Powell- Hyde Line at Powell and Market streets passing through the twisty Lombard Street and popular Russian Hill neighborhood.

Chinatown: You may have visited Chinatown in other towns. However, San Francisco's Chinatown is in a league by itself. It is the biggest Chinatown outside of Asia as well as the oldest in North America. Chinatown, which was almost totally damaged in the 1906 earthquake, was reconstructed totally in Chinese fashion and quickly became more appealing than before the tragedy. It hosts one of the largest Asian communities outside of Asia, and is considered one the oldest in North America. The best way to explore this neighborhood is to wander around on foot. Grand Avenue is the most tourist-heavy spot in Chinatown serving thousands of visitors each day. Also make sure to buy souvenirs for your loved ones in your family.

When to visit San Francisco?

From September to November is the most satisfactory period to tour San Francisco. Fall, believe it or not, has some of the town's hottest temperatures throughout the year, as well as fewer crowds than summertime. Spring is also a significant period to travel because of the mild weather and lack of rain (compared to other parts of California).

How to get around in San Francisco?

Taxi: Unlike in other main U.S. towns, such as New York, cabs in San Francisco are often not simple to locate, let alone grab. If you want to get somewhere rapidly, call ahead or use a ride-sharing application like Uber or Lyft.

Car: To discover central San Francisco, you don't require a vehicle; most of the major attractions are within strolling distance or are quickly accessible by Muni.
