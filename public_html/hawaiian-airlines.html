General Conditions of Acceptance of Baggage

Your baggage will be accepted onboard the Hawaiian Airlines flight cabin as per the given conditions:

  1. Valid Ticket: You must present a valid ticket for transportation on Hawaiian flights or 'Ohana by Hawaiian and one or more other carriers with which Hawaiian has a codeshare agreement or interline transportation.
  2. Same Flight: Your baggage will be transported on the same flight that you have book with Hawaiian. Any flight other than the one you are scheduled on may not accept your baggage for transportation.
  3. Name and Contact Details Required: Each item of checked baggage must have your name and a phone number. Hawaiian Airlines will provide you a free identification label if you don't have one already.
  4. Size and Weight Limits: Your checked bag must not exceed the total linear dimensions of 62 in (157 cm) or the maximum weight of 50 lbs (22.5 kg). However, Hawaiian Airlines may accept larger or heavier baggage after the payment of additional overweight or oversize baggage fees.
  5. Inspection: Hawaiian and/or any other authorized government authorities may inspect your luggage with or without your knowledge or consent. If you refuse to allow them to inspect your luggage, the authorities reserve the right to remove it or refuse to transport it.
  6. Suitable for Transportation: Hawaiian's sole discretion requires that your luggage be fit for travel. The airline won't take any baggage that:
    • is prohibited by an Government Law;
    • is not suitable to carry safely in the baggage compartment of the flight;
    • may be a reason to cause injury or damage, or pose a safety risk to Hawaiian, its staff, cabin crew, guests, any aircraft or other equipment, or luggage, due to its packaging, contents, nature, or characteristics, or;
    • is not suitable or sufficiently protected to withstand normal handling, unless the guest signs a release form.
  7. Valid Destination: Your luggage must be checked through a valid airport or destination. It will not be checked if:
    • To a point that is not on your booking route.
    • If there is no Stopover after the final destination listed on your ticket, after your next point of Stopover.
    • Beyond the point when you would like to reclaim all or some of your luggage.
    • Beyond the point at which you have cleared all applicable charges.
    • If the connecting flight is scheduled to depart from a different airport than the one at which you are scheduled to arrive, after the point at which you transfer to the connecting flight.

Hawaiian Airlines Pet Travel Policy

Hawaiian Airlines is one of the trusted airline companies of the United States of America and is based in the US state of Hawaii. It is known for its customer-friendly policies and offers a convenient in-flight environment to everyone. Book Hawaiian Airlines Flight Tickets from the official website and get the best travel experiences onboard.

Hawaiian allows its passengers to travel with their pets without worrying about anything and ensures a comfortable flight journey for them and their companions. They accommodate dogs and cats in a pressurized, temperature-controlled cargo compartment of the aircraft following the policy stated below.

Traveling with Pet in the Passenger Cabin
  • Passengers can travel with their pets only on inter-island flights and between the State of Hawaii and North America (except flights to/from JFK, BOS, MCO or AUS).
  • Only dogs and cats are permitted onboard.
  • The pet carrier should be fit properly under the front seat.
Traveling with Pet as Checked-In Baggage
  • The combined weight of the pet and kennel must not exceed 70 lbs. If the weight exceeds 70 lbs., it may be transported via the cargo department.
  • The pet will be loaded in the baggage compartment of the Hawaiian Airlines flight.
  • Passengers can book their pet as checked-in baggage at the same time of their flight reservations (subject to applicable fees and space availability)
Traveling with Service Animals

Passengers may travel with a service animal that has been trained to perform specific work or tasks to assist a person with a disability in the management of their disability. It must be properly vaccinated, fully trained, and well-weaned.

Traveling with Emotional Support Animals

Effective Jan. 11, 2021, Hawaiian Airlines no longer accepts emotional support animals as service animals, following the U.S. Department of Transportation's updated regulations (14 CFR Part 382). Emotional support animals may be recognized and transported as pets if they meet all pet acceptance requirements.

Hawaiian Airlines FAQs

Q. How to book First Class with Hawaiian Airlines?

Ans. If you want to upgrade your existing Hawaiian Airlines reservation to first/business class, you can change your reservation through online or kiosk check-in. Confirmed upgrades to First/Business class subjected to applicable fare difference.

Q. How much is the cost to fly on a first-class ticket of Hawaiian airlines?

Ans. The cost of a first-class ticket depends on the duration of the route. You can expect its fare between $1,100 to $3,000 for round-trip first-class seats on non-stop flights between San Francisco and all the major airports in Hawaii.

Q. How do I change my Hawaiian Airlines flight?

Ans. Simply follow the below steps to change Hawaiian flights through the official website:

  • Visit Hawaiian Airlines' official website and click on the 'My Trips' tab appearing on the homepage.
  • Enter your last name and confirmation code or ticket number in the boxes and then select "View My Trip."
  • Select "Change Flights" and then select the passengers whose flights need to be changed.
  • Select the flight(s) you want to change, then choose your new flight(s) and complete your itinerary selections.
Q. How can I manage my Hawaiian Airlines Flight Booking?

Ans. You can manage Hawaiian airlines flights, make changes to your flights and seats, buy an upgrade, track your flight status, and much more. Visit the 'Manage your Trip' section and fill in the required information like your Last Name and the Confirmation Code. Then, you will be directed to the page where you can change or view your itinerary.

Q. How do I print my boarding pass?

Ans. Hawaiian Airlines offers three-way check-in for flights where you can avoid long queues, viz, online, mobile, and airport check-in.

Online Check-In: You can check in using your computer or laptop. Go to the 'Manage Flights' section, choose the 'Check-in' option from the dropdown menu. Enter the requisite details, follow the instructions and get your boarding pass in less time.

Mobile App Check-In: Download the official mobile application of Hawaiian Airlines and check in with your smartphone. Go to the 'Manage My Trip' section, enter the requisite details for the mobile check-in and get your boarding pass on your registered email id.

Airport Check-In: you can check-in at the airport using Hawaiian self-service kiosks. Enter your confirmation code, confirmation email, and E-Ticket/Hawaiian Miles number, to proceed with the check-in process, and then print your boarding pass.

Q. Can I get any vacation offers from Hawaiian Airlines?

Ans. Yes, the airline company provides you with the option of awesome vacation packages. There are various choices such as Flight & Hotel; Flight & Car; Flight, Hotel, & Car; Hotel & Car or Hotel only. It will save you time and money by giving you a complete package, including hotel, car, and air travel.

Q. How do I upgrade my first class with Hawaiian Miles?

Ans. You can use your Hawaiian Miles by contacting the Hawaiian Airlines Reservations counter. However, Hawaiian Miles upgrades must be completed no later than 26 hours before the scheduled flight departure.

Q. What document do I need to carry for my Hawaiian airline flights?

Ans. Domestic Travel: Documents for travel to/from Hawaii and within the Hawaiian Islands are the same as any other U.S. state. If you are flying to Hawaii within the United States, you only need a valid government photo ID.

Children and teenagers under 18 do not need an ID when traveling with a companion who has a valid ID but they must be traveling with some form of ID, such as a school ID card.

International Travel: Travelers (adults, infants, and children) must have a valid U.S. passport when traveling internationally. A visa may also be required for entry at your destination airport.
